Project Overview

The Gaza Strip faces one of the world's most critical water crises, with over 90% of the population lacking reliable access to safe drinking water. Years of conflict, damaged infrastructure, fuel shortages, and over-extraction of groundwater have left desalination plants and sewage treatment systems barely functional. This has resulted in severe water insecurity, the spread of waterborne diseases, and heavy dependence on humanitarian aid.


Our project addresses this crisis by developing an educational and solution-driven digital platform. The platform integrates NASA Earth observation datasets with humanitarian reports to visualize water scarcity, pollution levels, and infrastructure status in Gaza. Through interactive dashboards and simplified data stories, it aims to raise awareness, support decision-making, and empower communities.
