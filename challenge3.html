Recycling Rubble: A Community Solution for Gaza

Problem Statement

Gaza faces massive infrastructure damage due to repeated demolitions and bombings. Entire neighborhoods have been reduced to rubble, leaving behind enormous amounts of construction waste. At the same time, access to fresh construction materials is extremely limited due to restrictions and high costs. This has created a dual crisis: huge piles of debris harming the environment and public safety, and a shortage of affordable building materials for reconstruction. Communities urgently need practical, local solutions that transform this rubble into resources for rebuilding homes, schools, hospitals, and public spaces.

Potential Solutions & Innovations

Gaza's rubble is more than waste it's a resource waiting to be transformed. By recycling debris into usable materials, communities can rebuild homes, schools, and hospitals while creating jobs, reducing costs, and turning destruction into an opportunity for resilience.
