Emergency Health Response Plan

Problem Statement

Gaza is facing a critical healthcare emergency. Overcrowding, conflict damage, and lack of resources have devastated the health system. Mortality rates are rising because many injuries and illnesses go untreated. With 75% of water wells destroyed and fuel shortages preventing the remaining wells from operating, communities are suffering from severe water scarcity. Poor sanitation and contaminated water increase the risk of deadly outbreaks such as diarrhea, cholera, and respiratory diseases. At the same time, children and vulnerable groups are experiencing acute malnutrition and dehydration. Hospitals are either destroyed or overwhelmed, with shortages of medicines, oxygen, electricity, and surgical tools. Mental health needs are escalating due to trauma, displacement, and loss, especially among children. Without immediate action, Gaza faces not only a medical collapse but also an epidemic and humanitarian disaster.

Potential Solutions & Innovations

In emergencies where health systems collapse, saving lives and preventing further suffering require rapid, coordinated interventions. The following solutions focus on immediate care, outbreak prevention, nutrition, and the restoration of essential health services. They balance short-term survival measures with longer-term recovery, ensuring communities regain access to safe water, food, medical care, and psychological support.

Immediate Care, Public Health & Recovery

  • Save lives through triage & first aid.
  • Prevent disease outbreaks.
  • Restore basic health, nutrition, and WASH (water, sanitation, hygiene).
  • Build temporary → long-term medical services.
  • Support mental health & train local staff.

1. Mortality Management

  • Train teams for safe, dignified recovery & burial.
  • Document & identify bodies using WHO/ICRC guidelines.
  • Communicate clearly: bodies are not epidemic sources.

2. Outbreak Detection & Control

  • Create Rapid Response Teams (RRT).
  • Daily clinic surveillance (diarrhea, fever, respiratory).
  • Test water & food, chlorinate water, promote hygiene.
  • Isolate contaminated sources immediately.

3. Malnutrition & Dehydration

  • Screen children (MUAC) and pregnant/lactating women.
  • Treat with ORS, IV fluids, RUTF, therapeutic feeding.
  • Support breastfeeding & set up feeding centers.

4. Temporary Tent Hospitals

  • Large durable weather-resistant tents
  • Internal sections (triage, emergency, surgery, maternity, ICU, pharmacy)
  • Ensure supplies: fuel, oxygen, medicines, blood bank.

5. Triage Prioritization

  • Red (Immediate): life-threatening, urgent transfer.
  • Yellow (Urgent): serious but stable.
  • Green (Minor): ambulatory, treat & discharge.
  • Grey (Expectant): palliative care if survival is unlikely.

6. Mental Health & Psychosocial Support

  • Train responders in Psychological First Aid (PFA).
  • Child-friendly safe spaces for families.
  • Referral pathways for PTSD, depression, psychosis.

7. Essential Supplies Needed

  • Field hospital kits, surgical tools, oxygen.
  • IV fluids, antibiotics, pain relief.
  • RUTF, ORS, zinc for children.
  • Water purification tablets, generators, solar kits.
  • PPE, wound care kits, sterilization.
